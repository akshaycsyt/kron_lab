//_OPERATOR_GUIDE

*

The Wildcard

Means "every" value. An asterisk in the Hour field means "every hour".

?

No Specific Value

Used when you don't want to specify a value for Day or Weekday. Essential for Spring Boot.

/

Increments

Used for intervals. 0/15 means "starting at 0, every 15 units".

//_COMMON_SCRIPTS

Top of the Hour

0 0 * * * ?

Runs exactly at the start of every hour.

Business Hours

0 0 9-17 * * MON-FRI

Every hour from 9 AM to 5 PM, weekdays only.

Every 15 Minutes

0 0/15 * * * ?

Quarter-hour intervals (0, 15, 30, 45).

//_HISTORY

Cron is derived from word 'Chronos' which is Greek word for time.

It is a time-based job scheduler in Unix-like computer operating systems. It is used to schedule jobs to run at specified times or intervals.

Also used in backend applications like Spring Boot to schedule jobs to run at specified times or intervals.

REMEMBER: SPRING BOOT CRON HAS 6 FIELDS. TRADITIONAL LINUX CRON HAS 5.
